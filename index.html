I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at the University of California, Berkeley. I study disability politics, social movements, and human rights activism in African countries. My research examines how disability rights movements emerge, how advocacy organizations make claims on the state, and how official statistics shape political recognition.

Research

My dissertation examines the emergence and effectiveness of disability rights movements in Africa, with a focus on how disabled persons organizations have shaped state recognition through national censuses, rights legislation, and social protection systems.

My broader research interests include African politics, disability politics, political representation, state legibility, social movements, and comparative politics.

Teaching

I have taught and supported courses in quantitative methods, comparative politics, African politics, and research design.
